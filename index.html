-A structure where two common emitter (CE) stages are connected in series to greatly amplify a signal.
-C1–C3 are coupling capacitors that pass AC signals while blocking DC.
-The input signal at the base of the first stage is amplified at the collector.
-Re provides DC stabilization and increases AC gain.
-C4 and C5 are bypass capacitors that remove AC negative feedback to maximize voltage gain.

A stereo audio amplifier is a device that amplifies a small audio signal
and outputs it to two speakers. The input audio signal passes through
resistors that adjust frequency characteristics and capacitors that
block DC, then its gain is increased by an op-amp IC chip.
The volume can be adjusted with a variable resistor,
and the strength of the input signal varies depending
on the position of the center terminal.
